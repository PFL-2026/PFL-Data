Editorial judgement
vs the market

Both lists are credible. They're built on completely different inputs — which is exactly why comparing them is useful.

How we rank today

PFL editorial rankings

A committee top-10 per division, anchored to the current champion and refreshed by hand after events.

  • Champion-led — the belt holder sits at the top by status.
  • Curated — ~88 fighters across the men's divisions, women's flyweight and pound-for-pound.
  • Human — reflects narrative and priorities, but the reasoning isn't shown.
What the data adds

SWA market model

An opponent-adjusted rating for every fighter, derived from pre-fight and in-play betting markets via Sportradar.

  • Performance-led — status aside, the body of work drives the number.
  • Full coverage — 357 fighters across every weight band, prospects included.
  • Auditable & auto-updating — one transparent number, re-rated after every event.

PFLPart 2 · MatchmakingHow a fight is scored

What makes a good fight

Every pairing gets a Grade out of 100 — a scouting judgement of fight quality, weighing four things. Pick a grade to see how it's built.

The four axes
  • Balance — how competitive; even matchups score highest.
  • Intrigue — stylistic interest; striker vs grappler scores high.
  • Stakes — does winning move them up the division.
  • Safety — avoids a bad look; one-sidedness or big gaps reduce it.
80–100Make itstrong on every axis
60–79Goodcompromised on one thing
40–59Marginalone factor dragging it down
0–39Avoida mismatch or low-value pairing

The Grade is fight quality, not a winner prediction — the model's win % sits beside it. The database is unisex, so a manual sex-check is applied before any pairing is booked.

PFLPart 3 · ScoutingSWA's scouting model

Finding champions before they're champions

This is SWA's scouting model — not something PFL runs today. Here's what it does and how we could use it. It plots a prospect's cumulative rank gain against their fight number, then lays it over the PFL-champion benchmark: the career trajectory a fighter who becomes a PFL champion has typically followed.

How to read it
  • The dashed line is a PFL champion's path. It's the trajectory our champions have followed, fight by fight, in SWA's scouting reports.
  • On or above the line is the signal. A prospect tracking the benchmark — or beating it early — is climbing the way a future champion does.
  • Slice by age or division. Brackets normalise for career stage; divisions show who's leading a weight class.
Why it matters for us. It's SWA's data, not ours yet — but if we want to find the next PFL champion, this is the exact shape to look for. Records flatter everyone; the benchmark separates "undefeated" from "undefeated, and climbing like a champion."

PFLBeyond rankings & matchmaking

Where this goes next

Rankings and matchmaking are the obvious first two. The same data spine quietly powers a lot more of the business — a few places worth exploring.

Card construction

Build whole events to maximise total fight quality — balance grades across main card and prelims.

Title-shot justification

A defensible, data-backed answer to "why this #1 contender?" — for media, managers and fans.

Fight location selection

We're looking into more data to support where we choose to go — layering market size, travel, venue and audience signals behind every host-city call.

Global talent pipelines

Rank and compare across regions to feed PFL's international cards with the right names at the right time.
